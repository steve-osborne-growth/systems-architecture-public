# About Me

I am a growth systems architect. That is not a title I have held on paper. It is the most accurate description of what I actually do.

---

## Background

I wrote my first line of HTML at 15. By high school I was building full websites in HTML, CSS, PHP, and JavaScript, not because someone told me to, but because I wanted to understand how things worked and then make them work better. That instinct has never left me.

Before working in tech full time, I owned and operated King's Escape Room in South Denver for five years. I built the rooms, the puzzles, the electronics, the marketing, the staff systems, and eventually the operating manual tight enough that I could hand the keys to a general manager and walk away. The business ran without me for over a year before I sold it for full asset value, including the SEO infrastructure that was still driving traffic. That experience shaped how I think about systems more than anything else in my career. A system that requires its architect to operate is not a system. It is a job.

When I moved back into tech, I kept building. At Trimble I have architected a customer intelligence platform that contributed $3M in estimated sales influence, rebuilt a fragmented billing program from $3.5M to $15M per year, designed a 5,500-customer licensing migration system, and built an executive intelligence platform that now delivers board-level narratives with real customer evidence every quarter.

In between, I have done freelance work: replaced a $900/month SaaS product for a personal training business with a custom-built system at hosting cost only, and before large language models existed, built a content variation and API scheduling system that generated $24K in monthly recurring revenue for an SEO agency within six months.

I have also led four company-wide presentations on prompt engineering and AI systems, won a Trimble Innovation Award for Project Crosstalk, and won a global hackathon with Project Compass.

---

## How I Work

I read a business problem, design a system that solves it, and build a working version, often before anyone else has finished debating whether it is worth trying. I operate with high autonomy. I do not need a complete specification to get started. I build to learn, iterate based on real feedback, and ship things that continue to generate value after I have moved on to the next problem.

Every project in this portfolio was architected by me. Some had collaborators. All had me driving direction, design, and delivery.

I am equally comfortable writing Python to connect APIs, engineering multi-pass prompts for AI analysis pipelines, designing SQL data flows in Domo, building Google Apps Script tools that sellers use daily, or presenting findings to senior leadership and board-level stakeholders. The problem determines the tool. I do not have a preferred hammer.

## What I Am Building Toward

I want to work where systems thinking and AI fluency meet real business outcomes. I am drawn to roles where the job is to figure out what should exist and then make it exist, not just execute a pre-defined roadmap.

The best systems I have built were not planned from day one. They started as a hypothesis, got tested against reality, and evolved into something more valuable than the original idea. Crosstalk started as a cross-sell tool and became an intelligence platform that powers board narratives. The On-Demand infrastructure I built for billing now powers a 5,500-customer licensing migration. The escape room operating system I built to step away from the business made it sellable.

That compounding, where a system built for one purpose ends up solving a harder problem, is what I am after. I want to be in an environment where that kind of thinking is the job.
