# The Transition: How the Business Ran Without Me

This document describes what had to be true for King's Escape Room to operate without the owner present, and how each condition was met.

---

## The Problem With Owner-Operators

Most small business owners are not building a business. They are building a job that only they can do. The owner is the product, the decision-maker, the quality control, and the face of the company all at once. When they step away, everything degrades.

E-Myth Revisited framed this clearly: a business should be a system of systems, not a person. Every role, every process, and every customer interaction should work the same way regardless of who is executing it. The owner's job is to design the system, not to be the system.

That reframe changed how every decision in the business was made.

---

## What Had to Be True

**All knowledge had to be documented.**
If the answer to any question lived only in the owner's head, the business was fragile. Every process, policy, customer question, equipment failure scenario, and escalation path had to be written down and findable.

**Employees had to be empowered to make decisions.**
A staff member who has to call the owner for every non-standard situation is not autonomous. Training had to go deep enough that employees could handle unexpected situations using good judgment and documented principles, not by escalating every edge case.

**Technology had to be reliable or have a documented failure protocol.**
A broken Arduino puzzle mid-session is a customer experience failure. Every piece of technology had a maintenance schedule and a documented workaround if it failed during a session.

**Customer acquisition had to run without the owner.**
SEO and the booking system ran continuously in the background. No owner involvement was required to bring customers in the door or confirm their reservation.

**A capable general manager had to own operations.**
The GM role was the capstone. Every system beneath the GM had to be solid enough that the GM could operate with full autonomy. The owner's job shifted from running the business to ensuring the systems that supported the GM were maintained and improved.

---

## How Long It Took

The transition did not happen at once. It happened in stages over several years as each system was built, tested, documented, and handed off. The owner operated the business at full involvement at the start. As each system matured, a layer of involvement was removed.

By the end, the owner had been working a separate full-time job for over a year while the escape room continued to generate revenue under the GM's management. When the sale came, the business transferred cleanly because the systems transferred with it.
