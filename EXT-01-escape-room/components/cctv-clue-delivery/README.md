# Component: CCTV Monitoring and Clue Delivery System

## Purpose

Give staff full visibility into every room from a central control point and deliver clues to customers immersively without breaking the in-room experience.

---

## What It Did

The original clue delivery method was a bell and a card slid under the door. Customers rang the bell when stuck, and staff slid a printed clue card into the room. It worked but broke the fourth wall completely. The moment a card appeared under the door, the illusion of being inside a real world ended.

The replacement system had two parts: a CCTV network covering every room and a digital clue delivery system that pushed content directly to in-room televisions.

Staff monitored all rooms simultaneously from the control room. Customers used an RFID-based buzzer to signal for help. The buzzer identified which room was requesting a clue, and staff could see exactly where the customer was and what they were working on via the CCTV feed.

Clues were pre-built for every puzzle in every room, including branching variations for different scenarios. Staff selected the appropriate clue and delivered it in one click. The clue appeared on the in-room TV as if it were part of the experience.

---

## Built With

- Hardwired CCTV cameras throughout all rooms feeding into a central monitor array
- RFID-based buzzer system identifying which room was signaling
- Chromecast devices in each room receiving the clue broadcast
- Open source broadcast software modified to support one-click delivery to specific rooms
- Pre-built clue library covering all puzzle scenarios and common stuck points

---

## Impact

This system removed the last obvious seam between the staff and the customer experience. Customers could ask for help without feeling like they were breaking a rule or stepping outside the game. The clue arrived as part of the room, not as a note from the outside world.

It also made multi-room operation by a single employee practical. One staff member could monitor four rooms simultaneously, identify where each group was, and deliver targeted help without leaving the control room.
