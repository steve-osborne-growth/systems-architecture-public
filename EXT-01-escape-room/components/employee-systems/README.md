# Component: Employee Systems and Process Manuals

## Purpose

Document every process, expectation, and scenario an employee would encounter so the business could operate to a consistent standard without the owner present.

---

## What It Did

This was the hardest and most valuable system built in the entire business.

The documentation covered everything an employee touched across a full shift: room reset procedures after each session, the exact sequence of the customer welcome, key talking points for the story and safety briefing, how to run a session for a corporate event, how to operate up to four rooms simultaneously as one person, time tracking and policies, escalation procedures for equipment failures, and every common customer question and its answer.

The goal was to remove all ambiguity. An employee should never need to guess what to do, make something up, or wait for the owner. Every scenario that could reasonably occur had a documented answer.

---

## Built With

- Written process manuals covering all operational scenarios
- Training videos demonstrating correct procedures
- A digital clue delivery system so staff could assist customers without entering rooms
- A closed-circuit CCTV system so staff could monitor all rooms from one location
- Repeated hands-on practice sessions before employees worked independently
- A management layer that balanced standardization with personal style: employees were expected to hit every key touchpoint but were given latitude in how they delivered them

---

## Impact

This system made the GM role viable. Without fully documented processes beneath her, a general manager would have required constant owner input. With them, the GM could make decisions, train new staff, and handle operations without escalating to the owner.

The documentation also made the business sellable. A buyer purchasing a business where all knowledge lived in the owner's head is buying a job. A buyer purchasing a business where every system is documented is buying a business. That distinction directly affected the sale value.
