# Component: SEO-Driven Marketing System

## Purpose

Build and maintain the organic search presence that drove nearly all customer acquisition, without relying on paid advertising or third-party platforms.

---

## What It Did

The entire revenue engine of the business ran on organic search. Customers searching for escape rooms in the Denver area found King's Escape Room at the top of Google search results and Google Maps. That ranking was not accidental. It was built and maintained through a deliberate, consistent content and citation strategy.

The system had several components:

**Content publishing:** A consistent cadence of articles and pages targeting search terms relevant to the business: team building ideas, school fundraiser options, things to do in South Denver, how to solve escape rooms, reviews of other local rooms, corporate event planning guides. Each piece served both the reader and the search algorithm.

**Google Maps and local SEO:** Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) listings across every relevant directory. Active review generation through post-session email requests. Maintaining accurate and complete Google Business Profile information at all times.

**Technical SEO:** The website was custom-coded in HTML, PHP, CSS, and JavaScript. Page structure, load speed, internal linking, and schema markup were all managed in-house. No website builder or template platform was used.

**Review management:** Google reviews fed directly back into local search rankings. The post-session email system consistently requested reviews, and staff were trained on how to encourage customers to leave them organically.

---

## Built With

- Custom website in HTML, PHP, CSS, and JavaScript
- Google Search Console and Analytics for performance tracking
- Citation management across local directories
- Internally trained staff who contributed SEO content as part of their role

---

## Impact

Organic search as the primary acquisition channel meant the business had extremely low customer acquisition costs compared to competitors running paid advertising. High margins were partly a function of not paying for traffic.

The SEO infrastructure was also a key asset in the sale. A business with documented, transferable search rankings and a content system that continues generating traffic is worth more than one where marketing knowledge lives only with the owner.
