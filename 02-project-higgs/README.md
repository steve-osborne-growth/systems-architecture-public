# Project Higgs: Executive Intelligence Platform

Adding real customer evidence and context to board narratives, QBR materials, and deal analysis so leadership sees the "so what," not just the data.

*Named after the Higgs boson discovery: a theory believed to be true but requiring evidence to confirm. This system finds the evidence.*

---

## The Problem

Trimble's QBRs, board narratives, and MD&A materials contained plenty of data points. Deals closed. Revenue numbers. Pipeline figures. But when these materials were presented to external audiences like board members, they were met with blank stares. The data did not mean anything without context.

A list of closed deals does not tell a board member why those deals matter. A revenue number does not explain whether the company's strategic initiatives are working. The missing layer was the "so what" analysis: why does this deal matter, who cares, and what does it tell us about the direction of the business?

Additionally, there was no evidence-based system to prove that Trimble's customer adoption initiative was working. Leadership believed it was, but belief is not evidence. Real, candid customer quotes showing the initiative in action were needed.

---

## Outcomes

- Board materials now include a customer evidence section that leadership looks forward to each quarter
- Candid, non-marketing customer quotes provide real proof of the initiative working
- Deal analysis now includes context on why a deal matters, not just that it happened
- Each quarterly iteration runs faster than the last as processes tighten
